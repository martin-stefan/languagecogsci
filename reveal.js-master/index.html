This video showed a few good examples of how different languages allow people to think differently about certain events

However, does this mean that they people perceive the world differently based on what language they speak?

This brings up the Sapir Whorf hypothesis
This hypothesis states that language determines thought. Using an example from the video, the Sapir Whorf hypothesis would claim that Russians are capable of recognizing different colors of blue because of the language they speak.
If this claim were true, this could provide many different implications of the abilities of humans and it could mean that speaking another language "unlocks" some sort of ability.

This sounds too good to be true.
It is.
The Sapir Whorf hypothesis struggled to provide much evidence that would strengthen their claim and other researchers found evidence which contradicts it.
It seems then that language does not actually change how we see the world, however it allows us to describe the world differently.
